What is ZATCA Phase 2?

The Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) of Saudi Arabia introduced a mandatory e-invoicing (Fatoorah) framework in two phases. Phase 2 — the Integration Phase — requires businesses to connect their ERP or billing systems directly to the FATOORA portal via API for real-time invoice clearance and reporting.

Every B2B tax invoice and credit/debit note must be cryptographically signed, encoded in UBL 2.1 XML format, and transmitted to ZATCA before or at the point of issuance. Non-compliance can result in significant penalties, operational disruptions, and loss of business in Saudi Arabia.

ZATCA Phase 2 rollout began in January 2023 and continues in waves based on company revenue thresholds. If your annual revenue exceeds SAR 3 million, your integration deadline may already be active.

Phase 1

Generation Phase — businesses must generate structured e-invoices locally. Already mandatory.

Phase 2

Integration Phase — real-time FATOORA portal connectivity, digital signing, and XML clearance. Rolling out now.

Key requirement: Cryptographic stamp (CSID), QR code generation, UBL 2.1 XML format, and direct API integration with ZATCA FATOORA portal.

What is ZATCA Phase 2 e-invoicing?

ZATCA Phase 2 (Integration Phase) requires businesses in Saudi Arabia to integrate their ERP or billing systems directly with the ZATCA FATOORA portal for real-time clearance or reporting of e-invoices. Unlike Phase 1 (Generation Phase), Phase 2 mandates cryptographic signing and direct API connectivity with ZATCA. Non-compliance carries fines starting at SAR 5,000.
